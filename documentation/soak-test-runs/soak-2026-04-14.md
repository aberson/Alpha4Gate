## Key finding: the 60% was not learning

The single most important datapoint: **run 2 cycle 1 hit 5%, not 60%**.

Run 1 cycle 1's 60% was not a PPO learning outcome. If it were, the
policy-loaded start of run 2 would replicate or exceed it. Instead, the
just-trained policy scored 5%. Therefore cycle 1's 60% was one of:

1. RNG variance over 10 games (10 wins at true-mean-30% = p≈0.04, plausible)
2. Fresh-policy rule-engine fallback dominating early decisions before PPO
   takes over
3. Map/opponent seed luck

Across 90 non-cycle-1-run-1 games we observed 8W / 82L ≈ 9% — consistent with
the converged policy's behavior. The 60% is the outlier.

## Conclusion

Pure-PPO training at difficulty 5 with the current 63-rule reward shaping
**does not learn**. It regresses to a policy that loses ~95% of the time,
below the rules-only baseline.

Plausible causes:

1. **Signal sparsity.** At ~10% win rate the macro win/loss signal is too
   sparse; PPO learns primarily from shaping rewards, which at diff 5 may
   reward behaviors that look right mid-game but lose.
2. **Rule validation gap.** The 13 new rules added by advised run 20260414-0017
   were validated at diff 3. They may shape incorrectly at diff 5 where tempo,
   scouting, and response windows differ.
3. **Training-distribution mismatch.** The bot never experienced diff 5 in
   training before; imitation data (if any) is from diff 1-3. PPO has no prior
   to anchor to.

## Recommendations

1. **Next soak should pin diff 3**, not diff 5. That is the baseline where we
   have 75%+ win rate — PPO will have a mix of wins/losses to learn from, and
   the new rules can be validated against a working policy.
2. **Re-audit the 13 new reward rules** in context of diff 5 play. Memory
   `project_advised_run_20260413_1601.md` notes the advised run hit 100% at
   diff 3 — those rules might be overfitted to the diff-3 opponent's patterns.
3. **Consider a curriculum soak** (diff 3 → auto-advance to 4 at 80% WR) rather
   than pinning, to let PPO build up macro signal before being thrown at diff 5.
4. **Do not trust cycle-1 win rates** as a learning signal — 10 games is too
   small to distinguish a 60% policy from a 30% one.
