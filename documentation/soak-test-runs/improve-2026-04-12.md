## Primary finding — PPO policy never attacks; every game times out at 480s

All 13 training games hit the 480s timeout. The bot spams `expand_now()` until it runs out of expansion locations (dozens of "Trying to expand_now() but bot is out of locations to expand to" warnings per game), then stalls. The PPO policy never learns to build military units or attack.

### Why the timeout penalty alone doesn't fix this

The gradient timeout penalty fires correctly (-20 for no army, -80 for idle army). But **every game receives the same -20 penalty**, which means every trajectory looks equally bad from PPO's perspective — there's no gradient signal pointing toward "build an army and attack." The penalty tells PPO "don't stall" but doesn't tell it "attack."

### Root cause candidates (ordered by plausibility)

1. **Action space doesn't include "attack" as a learnable action.** If the PPO action space maps to strategic states (expand, tech, defend) but not to a direct "attack" action, the policy literally cannot learn to attack. Need to verify `_ACTION_TO_STATE` mapping in `environment.py`.
2. **Reward shaping rewards economy over aggression.** The `reward_rules.json` may heavily weight resource collection, structure count, and tech advancement — all of which the bot achieves by expanding. Military production and attacking give no intermediate reward.
3. **No intermediate combat reward.** PPO only gets a terminal signal (win/loss/timeout). Without per-step rewards for army supply, enemy units killed, or damage dealt, the 1500-step trajectories are too sparse for credit assignment.
4. **Advisor commands not influencing PPO actions.** The bridge delivers strategy commands from Claude, but these may not translate into the PPO action space. The bot might ignore advisor suggestions during training.

### Recommended next actions

1. **Investigate PPO action space** — verify `_ACTION_TO_STATE` includes attack-oriented actions. If not, add "build army" and "attack" actions.
2. **Add intermediate combat rewards** — per-step rewards for army supply, enemy units killed, damage dealt. This gives PPO a gradient toward military production.
3. **Lower `games_per_cycle`** or increase `total_timesteps` — with 1500 timesteps and ~120 steps per game, PPO is learning from ~12 games of identical timeout trajectories. Consider fewer games with more diverse initial conditions.
4. **Consider curriculum shaping** — start training with a pre-trained "always attack at 4 min" policy so PPO sees what winning looks like, then fine-tune.

## Secondary findings

### Advisor bridge only fires during first game of each cycle

The bridge queued 8 requests, all during game 1 of cycle 1 (~00:52–01:02). Games 2-10 of cycle 1 and games 1-3 of cycle 2 produced zero advisor requests. This may be by design (rate limiting, or advisor only consulted once per cycle) or a bug where the bridge's rate limiter doesn't reset between games.

### `expand_now()` spam

Every game produces dozens of "Trying to expand_now() but bot is out of locations to expand to" warnings. The PPO policy is heavily biased toward expansion actions. This is consistent with reward shaping that rewards economy/structures.

### Cycle 1 took 13 games, not 10

Despite `games_per_cycle=10`, cycle 1 played 13 games before completing. This is because `total_timesteps=1500` is the PPO budget, and each game produces ~120 timesteps. 1500/120 ≈ 12.5 games to exhaust the budget. The `games_per_cycle` config is misleading — it's not a hard cap.
